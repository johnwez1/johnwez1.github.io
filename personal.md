# Personal

Beyond technical work, I spend a large amount of time outdoors and in mountain environments.
This page is intended as a slower, more personal collection of field notes, photography,
and experiences gathered away from the screen.

<div class="feature-block">
<h2>Hiking</h2>

Multi-day hiking trips have become one of the best ways to disconnect from constant noise
and return to longer attention spans. Most trips revolve around remote glens, ridge traverses,
alpine lakes, and quiet paths that encourage slower movement and observation.

Future plans include adding route notes, maps, seasonal observations, and photography galleries.
</div>

<div class="feature-block">
<h2>Skiing</h2>

Skiing has shaped much of how I think about terrain, weather, and movement through landscapes.
I enjoy both resort skiing and backcountry touring, particularly in places where the environment
feels expansive and minimally developed.

Over time, this section will include trip journals, snow conditions, equipment notes,
and photo collections from winter seasons.
</div>

<div class="feature-block">
<h2>Paragliding</h2>

Paragliding introduced an entirely different perspective on geography and weather systems.
Flying creates a strong awareness of air movement, terrain interaction, and changing conditions.

Some of the most memorable experiences have come from calm evening ridge flights,
long coastal glides, and quiet moments suspended above landscapes that are otherwise
impossible to appreciate from the ground.
</div>

<div class="feature-block">
<h2>Rewilding</h2>

I have a growing interest in ecological restoration and rewilding, particularly projects focused
on biodiversity recovery, native woodland regeneration, peatland restoration, and long-term
stewardship of natural systems.

This area combines practical environmental work with a broader interest in resilience,
sustainability, and designing systems that improve over time rather than degrade.
</div>

Photos, essays, maps, and field notes will be added here later.
